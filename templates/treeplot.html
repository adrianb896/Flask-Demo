Sample Classifier

The gini index is a measurement or a guess of the impurity of the data in a particular node or sample set of a particular user. A gini index of 0.0 is randomly predicted as pure. A random sample gini of 0.5 usually means that almost half the time the data can make false predictions. This type of classification problem is ideal for a Decision Tree Model.

The random sample sets are then classified as vectors of x being the amount of users(V#) 'Not Fraud' and the y being the amount of users 'Fraud' in terms of the column 'Class' as to what is most likely to be considered fraudulent transactions.
